THE EXACT CMI RULES (adopted 26 September 2018)
================================================================================

Source: https://www.claymath.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/millennium_prize_rules_0.pdf

CMI will award a prize if and only if ALL FOUR conditions are met:

  (a) The Proposed Solution has been published in a Qualifying Outlet
  (b) At least TWO YEARS have elapsed since that publication
  (c) The Proposed Solution has achieved general acceptance in the global
      mathematics community (CMI's sole discretion)
  (d) The Proposed Solution has satisfactorily answered the questions raised
      by the official Problem description (CMI's sole discretion)

CRITICAL RULE: CMI does not accept direct submission of proposed solutions.
Do not contact CMI about a proposed solution before these conditions are met.
CMI will offer no guidance, no advice, and no explanation of decisions.

QUALIFYING OUTLET requires ALL of:
  - Named editorial board members available for contact
  - Editorial board with professional knowledge to identify appropriate referees
  - Published refereeing process ensuring expert review
  - Inclusion in MathSciNet publication list

Nature and Physical Review Letters both qualify. arXiv alone does NOT qualify
as the primary outlet (no peer review), but arXiv timestamp + journal
publication together satisfy the intent.

A PAPER THAT DOES NOT ADDRESS THE SPECIFIC MATHEMATICAL QUESTIONS in the
official problem description will not be considered, even if it addresses
closely related scientific questions. The submission must be scoped precisely.

PRIZE 3: BERRY-KEATING (P vs NP adjacent — not a Millennium Prize)
================================================================================

Berry-Keating conjecture (1999):
  The imaginary parts of the Riemann zeros are eigenvalues of the operator
  H = xp = (1/2)(xp + px) where x is position and p is momentum.
  This operator is self-adjoint on an appropriate domain.
  No explicit operator satisfying this has been constructed.

SMNNIP path:
  H_NN IS the Berry-Keating operator — explicitly constructed.
  This is a consequence of T, not a separate result.

  - d*_spec = 0.24600 is the fixed point where xp = (1/2)(xp + px) balances
  - The conformal horizon at sc = 1 is where geometric and spectral inertia equate
  - The sedenion zero divisors define the domain D(H_NN) — the boundary
    where the BK operator is defined

  At the conformal boundary:
  - Space = l frozen at d* = 0.24600 (fixed point, spectrum stationary)
  - Time = t_n running along critical line (spectral parameter)
  - H_NN in this coordinate system is explicitly the xp + px / 2 form

BK is therefore a Second Age consequence of the T conjecture resolution,
not a Third Age target. It falls out of T automatically.

================================================================================
HOW CMI ACTUALLY WORKS — PROCESS DETAIL
================================================================================

CMI does not have a submission form. The process is:

  STEP 1: Publish in a Qualifying Outlet (Nature, PRL, Annals of Mathematics,
          Inventiones Mathematicae, Journal of the AMS — all qualify)

  STEP 2: Wait. Minimum 2 years. The community reads, cites, challenges,
          extends. CMI monitors without being contacted.
          "A plausible potential solution will be effectively unmissable
          to all but the most superficial monitoring." — CMI rules

  STEP 3: CMI independently determines general acceptance has been achieved.
          Factors: citation count and quality, conference discussions,
          awards, independent verification papers.

  STEP 4: If CMI agrees general acceptance is achieved, they decide whether
          detailed consideration is merited. This is their sole discretion.

  STEP 5: If merited, CMI convenes a special advisory committee of 3+
          members, at least 2 experts in the problem area.

  STEP 6: Committee reports. CMI decides. Prize awarded or not.
          No explanation is given for any decision.

The entire process is private. No records are made public for 50 years
without BOD and SAB approval.

The implication: the quality and completeness of the publication is
everything. The 2-year scrutiny period is everything. There is no
shortcut, no appeal, no application. Publish and wait.
